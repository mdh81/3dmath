Rotation About Arbitrary Axis

Derivation for the rotation equation

In this section we will derive the equation to rotate a vector V about another vector R in 3D space. Vector V’s projection on vector R is

We split V into two vector components V∥ and V⟂ that are parallel and perpendicular to R, respectively.

V =V∥+V⟂ (1)
V∥ =(V.R)R (2)
V⟂ =V−(V.R)R (3)

We can describe the rotation of V in the plane perpendicular to R

We can see when V is rotated about R by θ, the resulting vector is V′. Considering the vector projection of V′ on R

V′ =V∥′+V⟂′ (4)
Because V∥′=V∥
V′ =V∥+V⟂′ (5)
Substituting V∥ from 2
V′ =(V.R)R+V⟂′ (6)

V and R are known quantities. V′⟂ can be calculated by observing that V⟂ and B and are two mutually perpendicular plane vectors. Along with R they describe a coordinate frame, V⟂B⁢R that can be used to compute V⟂′. Since B is mutally perpendicular to V′⟂ and R, it can be expressed as

B =R×V⟂ (7)
Substituting V⟂ from 3
B =R×(V−V∥) (8)
Since cross product distributes over vector subtraction and since V∥ and R are parallel
B =R×V−R×V∥ (9)
B =R×V−0 (10)
B =R×V (11)
Using V⟂ and B as basis vectors, V⟂′ can be written as
V⟂′ =V⟂⁢c⁢o⁢s⁢θ+(R×V)⁢s⁢i⁢n⁢θ (12)
Substituting V′⟂ into the equation 6, we get the final equation for V′ in terms of V and R
V′ =(V.R)R+V⟂cosθ+(R×V)sinθ (13)
V′ =(V.R)R+(V−(V.R)R)cosθ+(R×V)sinθ (14)

Note

There are three terms in 14

T⁢1 =(V.R)R (15)
T⁢2 =(V−(V.R)R)cosθ (16)
T⁢3 =(R×V)⁢s⁢i⁢n⁢θ (17)

Notice that T⁢2 is actually (V−T⁢1)⁢c⁢o⁢s⁢θ. We will be using these three terms in the derivation of the rotation matrix in the next section

Derivation for the rotation matrix

A column-major rotation matrix is of the form

[B⁢1B⁢2B⁢3]

The columns B⁢1,B⁢2, and B⁢3 are the coordinates of the basis vectors after rotation. To derive the rotation matrix for rotating V and producing V′, we start with the basis vectors of the right-handed coordinate system

[100010001]

We first rotate these basis vectors using the rotation equation (12), and to form the rotation matrix, we add the rotated coordinates of the basis vectors to the rotation matrix
